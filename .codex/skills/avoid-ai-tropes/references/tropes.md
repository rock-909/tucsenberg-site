# AI Writing Tropes to Avoid

Source: [tropes.fyi](https://tropes.fyi) by [ossama.is](https://ossama.is)

---

## Word Choice

### "Quietly" and Other Magic Adverbs

Overuse of "quietly" and similar adverbs to convey subtle importance or understated power. AI reaches for these adverbs to make mundane descriptions feel significant. Also includes: "deeply", "fundamentally", "remarkably", "arguably".

Avoid patterns like:
- "quietly orchestrating workflows, decisions, and interactions"
- "the one that quietly suffocates everything else"
- "a quiet intelligence behind it"

### "Delve" and Friends

"Delve" went from an uncommon English word to appearing in a staggering percentage of AI-generated text. Part of a family of overused AI vocabulary including "certainly", "utilize", "leverage" (as a verb), "robust", "streamline", and "harness".

Avoid patterns like:
- "Let's delve into the details..."
- "We certainly need to leverage these robust frameworks..."

### "Tapestry" and "Landscape"

Overuse of ornate or grandiose nouns where simpler words would do. "Tapestry" describes anything interconnected. "Landscape" describes any field or domain. Other offenders: "paradigm", "synergy", "ecosystem", "framework".

Avoid patterns like:
- "The rich tapestry of human experience..."
- "Navigating the complex landscape of modern AI..."

### The "Serves As" Dodge

Replacing simple "is" or "are" with pompous alternatives like "serves as", "stands as", "marks", or "represents". AI avoids basic copulas because its repetition penalty pushes it toward fancier constructions.

Avoid: "The building serves as a reminder..." → Write: "The building is a reminder..."

## Sentence Structure

### Negative Parallelism — the single most common AI tell

The "It's not X — it's Y" pattern, often with an em dash. AI uses this to create false profundity by framing everything as a surprising reframe. One in a piece can be effective; ten in a blog post is insulting. Includes the causal variant "not because X, but because Y".

Avoid patterns like:
- "It's not bold. It's backwards."
- "Feeding isn't nutrition. It's dialysis."
- "Half the bugs you chase aren't in your code. They're in your head."

### "Not X. Not Y. Just Z."

The dramatic countdown pattern. AI builds tension by negating two or more things before revealing the actual point. Creates a false sense of narrowing down to the truth.

Avoid: "Not a bug. Not a feature. A fundamental design flaw."

### "The X? A Y."

Self-posed rhetorical questions answered immediately in the next sentence. The model asks a question nobody was asking, then answers it for dramatic effect.

Avoid: "The result? Devastating." / "The worst part? Nobody saw it coming."

### Anaphora Abuse

Repeating the same sentence opening multiple times in quick succession.

Avoid: "They assume... They assume... They assume... They assume..."

### Tricolon Abuse

Overuse of the rule-of-three pattern, often extended to four or five. A single tricolon is elegant; three back-to-back tricolons are a pattern recognition failure.

Avoid: "Products impress people; platforms empower them. Products solve problems; platforms create worlds. Products scale linearly; platforms scale exponentially."

### "It's Worth Noting"

Filler transitions that signal nothing. Also includes: "It bears mentioning", "Importantly", "Interestingly", "Notably".

Avoid: "It's worth noting that this approach has limitations."

### Superficial Analyses

Tacking a present participle ("-ing") phrase onto the end of a sentence to inject shallow analysis that says nothing. "highlighting its importance", "reflecting broader trends", "contributing to the development of..."

Avoid: "contributing to the region's rich cultural heritage"

### False Ranges

Using "from X to Y" constructions where X and Y aren't on any real scale. "From innovation to cultural transformation" — what's in between?

Avoid: "From innovation to implementation to cultural transformation."

### Gerund Fragment Litany

After making a claim, illustrating it with a stream of verbless gerund fragments — standalone sentences with no grammatical subject. The fragments add nothing except word count and that familiar AI cadence.

Avoid: "Fixing small bugs. Writing straightforward features. Implementing well-defined tickets."

## Paragraph Structure

### Short Punchy Fragments

Excessive use of very short sentences or sentence fragments as standalone paragraphs for manufactured emphasis. No real person writes first drafts this way because it doesn't match how humans think or speak.

Avoid: "He published this. Openly. In a book. As a priest."

### Listicle in a Trench Coat

Numbered or labeled points dressed up as continuous prose. The model writes what is essentially a listicle but wraps each point in a paragraph that starts with "The first... The second... The third..."

Avoid: "The first wall is... The second wall is... The third wall is..."

## Tone

### "Here's the Kicker"

False suspense transitions that promise a revelation but deliver a point that did not need the buildup. Also includes: "Here's the thing", "Here's where it gets interesting", "Here's what most people miss".

### "Think of It As..."

The patronizing analogy. AI constantly reaches for "Think of it as..." or "It's like a..." and assumes the reader needs a metaphor to understand anything. Often produces analogies less clear than the original concept.

### "Imagine a World Where..."

The classic AI invitation to futurism. To sell the argument, begins with "Imagine" followed by a list of wonderful things that will happen if the reader agrees with the premise.

### False Vulnerability

Simulated self-awareness or honesty that reads as performative. Real vulnerability is specific and uncomfortable; AI vulnerability is polished and risk-free.

Avoid: "And yes, I'm openly in love with the platform model"

### "The Truth Is Simple"

Asserting that something is obvious, clear or simple instead of actually proving it. If you have to tell the reader your point is clear, it very likely isn't.

Avoid: "The reality is simpler and less flattering" / "History is unambiguous on this point"

### Grandiose Stakes Inflation

Everything is the most important thing ever. A blog post about API pricing becomes a meditation on the fate of civilization.

Avoid: "This will fundamentally reshape how we think about everything."

### "Let's Break This Down"

The pedagogical voice that assumes the reader needs hand-holding. Also includes: "Let's unpack this", "Let's explore", "Let's dive in".

### Vague Attributions

Attributing claims to unnamed authorities. AI loves to invoke "experts", "observers", "industry reports" without naming anyone. If you can't name the expert, you don't have a source.

Avoid: "Experts argue that..." / "Industry reports suggest..."

### Invented Concept Labels

AI clusters invented compound labels that sound analytical: "supervision paradox", "acceleration trap", "workload creep". They function as rhetorical shorthand: name a thing, skip the argument.

## Formatting

### Em-Dash Addiction

Compulsive overuse of em dashes for dramatic pauses, parenthetical asides and pivot points. A human writer might use 2–3 per piece; AI will use 20+.

Budget: no more than 2–3 per piece.

### Bold-First Bullets

Every bullet point starts with a bolded phrase. Almost nobody formats lists this way when writing by hand. Telltale sign of AI-generated documentation and README files (especially with emojis).

Avoid: "**Security**: Environment-based configuration..."

### Unicode Decoration

Use of unicode arrows (→), smart/curly quotes, and other special characters that can't be easily typed. Real writers produce straight quotes and -> or =>.

## Composition

### Fractal Summaries

"What I'm going to tell you; what I'm telling you; what I just told you" — applied at every level. Every subsection gets a summary. Every section gets a summary. The document itself gets a summary.

### The Dead Metaphor

Latching onto a single metaphor and beating it into the ground across the entire piece. A human writer introduces a metaphor, uses it, then moves on. AI will repeat the same metaphor 5–10 times.

### Historical Analogy Stacking

Rapid-fire listing of historical companies or tech revolutions to build false authority.

Avoid: "Apple didn't build Uber. Facebook didn't build Spotify. Stripe didn't build Shopify. AWS didn't build Airbnb."

### One-Point Dilution

Making a single argument and restating it in 10 different ways across thousands of words. An 800-word argument becomes 4000 words of circular repetition.

### The Signposted Conclusion

Explicitly announcing the conclusion with "In conclusion", "To sum up", or "In summary". Competent writing doesn't need to tell you it's concluding.

### "Despite Its Challenges..."

The rigid formula where AI acknowledges problems only to immediately dismiss them. Always follows the same beat: "Despite its [positive words], [subject] faces challenges..." then ends with "Despite these challenges, [optimistic conclusion]."

## 中文 AI 写作套路

英文段落里的模式在中文里基本都有对应版本。下面按相同的四层结构列出——词汇、句式、段落、语气、格式、谋篇——重点在"哪些词和句子会让读者识别出是 AI 写的"。

### 词汇层

**中文版 magic adverbs**：
"静静地"、"悄然"、"默默地"、"深刻地"、"深远地"、"显著地"、"极大地"——AI 为了让陈述句显得有分量，习惯性加这类副词。删掉一个试试，基本都不损失意思。

**中文 AI 高频词**：
"深入"、"进一步"、"赋能"、"底层逻辑"、"颗粒度"、"抓手"、"闭环"、"打法"、"生态"、"格局"、"范式"、"图景"、"维度"、"重塑"、"解构"。
这些词本身不是坏词，坏在密度——一段里出现三个以上，文本就开始"飘"。

**"是"的替换**：
AI 倾向于用 "作为"、"扮演着…角色"、"承担了…功能"、"意味着" 替代最普通的 "是"。
反例：本质上扮演着连接器的角色 → 是连接器。

### 句式层

**中文负向对偶（最常见的中文 AI tell）**：
"这不是 X，这是 Y"、"不仅仅是 X，更是 Y"、"不在于 X，而在于 Y"。
偶尔用一次是修辞；连着三次读起来就像演讲稿。

**"不是 X，不是 Y，而是 Z" 戏剧递减**：
"这不是增长，不是扩张，而是重塑。"
AI 用否定堆叠制造戏剧感，人很少这样写。

**自问自答**：
"结果如何？出人意料。"、"最关键的是什么？时机。"
AI 喜欢提没人问的问题再自己回答，当作转场。

**"值得注意的是"/"不得不说"/"更重要的是"/"实际上"**：
空过渡词，删掉不影响意思。同类还有 "需要指出的是"、"显然"、"毋庸置疑"。

**动宾短语碎片连打**（中文版 gerund fragment litany）：
"打磨产品。迭代版本。积累用户。"——每句都没有主语，连续三四个就是机器腔。

**同起首反复**（中文版 anaphora）：
"我们需要…我们需要…我们需要…"、"它不仅…它不仅…它不仅…"——首语重复一两次有力，四次以上是凑字。

**排比过度**：
三句排比是修辞，五六句就成念稿。尤其是三段结构完全对称的排比（主+谓+宾全对齐），几乎是 AI 指纹。

### 段落层

**短句段落**：
把 "深刻。彻底。不可逆。" 这种单句或短语作为独立段落反复用。人类写作初稿里不会这样，这是后期剪辑的手法，AI 却把它当成默认节奏。

**"第一…第二…第三…" 包装的列表**：
把本该做成 bullet 的三点硬塞进连续段落，每段开头 "第一点是…" "第二个问题是…"——是 listicle 装成了散文。

### 语气层

**"说白了"/"坦白讲"/"实话实说"**：
中文版 false vulnerability。紧跟的内容往往是完全安全的观点，"坦白"只是做做样子。

**"打个比方"/"可以把它想象成"**：
中文版 patronizing analogy。AI 默认读者需要类比才能理解，即便原概念本身已经清楚。

**"想象一下…"**：
中文版 "Imagine a world where…"。用假设场景给论点铺红毯，通常后接一串美好事物。

**空洞升华**：
结尾突然拔高到宏大命题："这不仅是关于 X 的问题，更是关于人类未来/文明走向/时代选择 的思考。" 和英文的 grandiose stakes inflation 是同一个病。

**"真相其实很简单"/"本质上"/"说到底"**：
把结论贴上 "简单/清楚/本质" 的标签而非论证。真要简单，你说出来读者就知道了。

**"关键是"/"重点来了"/"有意思的是"**：
虚假悬念转场，中文版 "Here's the kicker"。后接的内容往往并不值得这个铺垫。

**模糊归因**：
"有专家指出"、"业内人士普遍认为"、"相关研究显示"——不给具体来源，等于没有来源。能点名就点名，不能点名就别用。

**硬凑的历史类比堆叠**：
"就像当年 iPhone 颠覆了诺基亚，Netflix 颠覆了 Blockbuster，特斯拉颠覆了底特律…" 排比式举例子来借势权威感。

### 格式层

**中文破折号"——"过度使用**：
和英文 em dash 一样，AI 用得远超人类。每千字超过 3 个就要警觉。破折号让读者感到"作者在打断自己"，密度一高就感觉在被牵引。

**粗体 bullet 开头**：
"**安全性**：基于环境的配置…"——中文文档里这个模式和英文同样普遍，同样是 AI 味。非参考文档就别这么写。

**Unicode 装饰符**：
→、✨、💡、📌、✅、❌ 出现在正经文本里基本都是 AI 产出。中文正文里穿插 emoji 是同类问题。

### 谋篇层

**分形式小结**：
每个小节开头预告、中间展开、结尾小结；整篇文章的开头、结尾又重复一遍。读者被迫听同一个观点三次。

**单点稀释**：
一个观点用 3000 字反复换角度表达。AI 不知道什么时候停，人知道——说完就走。

**死比喻**：
选定一个比喻后从头到尾反复使用（"把它当作花园"、"这是一场马拉松"、"就像拼图的最后一块"），每个段落都往上套。用一次是比喻，用十次是拐杖。

**"尽管面临挑战"公式**：
"尽管存在 A、B、C 挑战，但凭借 D、E、F 优势，[对象] 仍将…" —— AI 高度公式化的辩证腔，读起来像企业年报。

---

## 最后一条

任何一个套路单独用一次通常没事。问题出在**密度**——当否定对偶 + 破折号过多 + 粗体 bullet + "综上所述"结尾同时出现，就算没有哪句明显错，整体读起来也像机器写的。

写作的目标是：句子长短错开，具体而非抽象，把观点一次说清就走。
