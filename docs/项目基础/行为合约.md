### Privacy and consent (owner decision, 2026-07)

Both buyer lead forms (Contact and Request a Quote) share the same
`InquiryForm` and express privacy the same way: a short privacy statement
rendered next to the submit button, sourced from the shared translation key
`inquiry.form.privacyNotice`, shown through the shared `FormPrivacyNotice`
component. There is no privacy consent checkbox on either form, and submission
is never gated by one — the only submit gate stays the Turnstile token.

The site does not run marketing email, so there is no marketing-consent field
anywhere: `marketingConsent` has been retired end-to-end (form, zod schemas,
`/api/contact` and `/api/inquiry`, lead pipeline, the Airtable "Marketing
Consent" column write, and the owner email templates). Proof: contact/RFQ form
and payload tests, lead-schema tests, Airtable create-operation tests, email
render tests, and the two E2E specs above.

## Lead pipeline details

Public lead entry points:

- Contact page → shared `InquiryForm` with `general-context` → `/api/inquiry` → `product` lead type with `productInquiryKind: "general-rfq"` (see BC-013A)
- Request a Quote page → server-validated `catalog-context | general-context` → shared `InquiryForm` → `/api/inquiry` → `catalog-product` or `general-rfq` (see BC-013 / BC-013A)
- `src/app/api/inquiry/route.ts` → the only lead-writing route; validates once and calls `processValidatedInquiry`
- `src/app/api/contact/route.ts` → temporary non-writing `410` tombstone owned for final removal by D6e

The inquiry route owns public buyer validation, Turnstile, honeypot handling, rate limiting, and the typed delivery call. Legacy contact frontend/config still exists in source for D6e retirement but is not a production write path.

The inquiry product identity is the source of truth for the owner email and
Airtable record: the display `Product Name` is resolved server-side (catalog
registry label, or a fixed general-RFQ label), `Product Slug` holds the
validated `catalogProductId` and is empty for a general RFQ, and `buyerInterest`
is folded into the description text only.

## Lead pipeline policy

The canonical policy, approved by the owner on 2026-07-07, is
`email-first-storage-optional`, implemented
by `LEAD_DELIVERY_POLICY` in `src/lib/lead-pipeline/process-lead.ts`: contact
and inquiry lead delivery starts owner email and Airtable persistence in
parallel, either channel succeeding is user-facing success, and both channels
failing returns failure. The design record is
`docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-06-lead-pipeline-parallel-delivery-design.md`.
Rationale: CRM degradation must not lose buyer submissions; Airtable gaps are
backfilled from the owner email using the logged `referenceId`.

The Airtable write is time-boxed by a request budget
(`AIRTABLE_REQUEST_TIMEOUT_MS` in `src/lib/airtable/service.ts`, 8000ms) applied
both at the Airtable SDK (`requestTimeout`) and as an in-process race in
`process-lead.ts`. This keeps `email-first` honest under a hung CRM: a buyer can
never wait minutes for Airtable. A contact or inquiry budget timeout degrades
to a non-blocking, logged failure and success stays email-driven.
